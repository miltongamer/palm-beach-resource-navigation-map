# Palm Beach Resource Navigation Map

A public-benefit resource-navigation project focused on homelessness, veteran services, behavioral health, substance use, housing instability, legal aid, transportation barriers, food support, hygiene services, reentry support, and crisis support in Palm Beach County, Florida.

This project exists because local service ecosystems are often rich in resources but poor in navigation. The goal is to turn scattered information into a practical referral tool that helps frontline workers, families, veterans, treatment providers, and people in crisis identify the right next step faster.

## Project description

This is an early-stage community infrastructure project. It is not yet a large open-source package or software dependency, but it is intended to become a maintained public resource for local service navigation.

The project organizes local resources into structured data and practical referral pathways. Instead of only listing phone numbers and websites, the goal is to explain what each resource does, who it serves, when to use it, what information should be verified, and what the next step should be.

## Who this is for

This project is designed to support:

- Homeless adults and families
- Veterans and veteran-serving providers
- Outreach workers
- Case managers
- Treatment providers
- Behavioral health and substance use professionals
- Legal aid and reentry partners
- Families trying to help someone in crisis
- Community organizations that need quick, practical referral information

## The problem

Palm Beach County has many helpful organizations, but the information is spread across county websites, nonprofit pages, VA resources, PDFs, crisis lines, community contacts, and informal referral networks. For someone facing homelessness, addiction, mental health needs, transportation barriers, legal issues, or financial instability, that fragmentation can delay care and lead to missed opportunities for help.

## The goal

The goal is to build and maintain a practical navigation map that includes:

- Resource names and categories
- Contact information
- Website/source links
- Service area
- Eligibility notes
- Best-use scenarios
- Referral steps
- Verification status
- Last-verified dates
- Confidence levels
- Notes for frontline users

## Current status

Early-stage. Initial structure and documentation are being built. Resource data should be independently verified before direct use with clients, patients, veterans, or families.

## Files in this repository

- `resources.csv` — Structured resource database template
- `referral-pathways.md` — Practical referral workflows for common scenarios
- `verification-log.md` — Log for tracking when resources were checked or updated
- `data-dictionary.md` — Explanation of fields used in the resource database
- `contributing.md` — Guidance for people who want to help improve the project
- `LICENSE` — Open-source license

## Responsible use notice

This project is for navigation and referral support only. It is not medical, legal, clinical, emergency, or crisis-care advice. Always verify service availability, eligibility, hours, and contact details directly with the listed organization before relying on the information.

For immediate danger or life-threatening emergencies, call 911. For suicide or mental health crisis support in the United States, call or text 988.
