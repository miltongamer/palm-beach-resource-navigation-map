# Referral Pathways

This file outlines practical referral pathways for common real-world situations. These pathways are intended to help frontline workers, families, veterans, and community partners identify the right next step quickly.

**Important:** Always verify resource availability, eligibility, hours, location, transportation options, and intake requirements before sending someone to a service.

---

## 1. Homeless adult needing shelter tonight

### Primary need
Immediate shelter, coordinated entry, food, hygiene, and safety planning.

### Suggested pathway
1. Confirm whether the person is in immediate danger or experiencing a medical or psychiatric emergency.
2. If immediate danger exists, call 911.
3. If there is suicide or mental health crisis risk, call or text 988.
4. Contact the local coordinated entry or homeless services access point.
5. Check emergency shelter availability and entry requirements.
6. Identify immediate food, hygiene, and transportation options.
7. Document the referral attempt, contact person, time, and outcome.

### Information to collect
- Name and callback number if available
- Current location
- Veteran status
- Family status / children present
- Disability or mobility needs
- Medical or behavioral health concerns
- ID status
- Transportation barriers

## 2. Veteran experiencing homelessness

### Primary need
Veteran-specific housing navigation, VA homeless services, emergency shelter, benefits connection, and behavioral health support if needed.

### Suggested pathway
1. Confirm immediate safety and crisis risk.
2. Ask whether the person is a veteran and whether they are enrolled in VA care.
3. Contact VA homeless services, HCHV, SSVF, Vet Center, or a local veteran-serving nonprofit as appropriate.
4. Check emergency shelter options while veteran-specific housing support is being explored.
5. If the veteran has substance use, mental health, or medical needs, connect with appropriate treatment or crisis services.
6. Document who was contacted and what the next step is.

### Key considerations
- Some veteran resources may require proof of service.
- VA enrollment is not always required for every veteran resource.
- Transportation can be a major barrier and should be addressed early.
- The person may need help obtaining documents.

## 3. Person needing detox or substance use treatment

### Primary need
Clinical screening, withdrawal safety, treatment placement, transportation, and step-down planning.

### Suggested pathway
1. Determine whether the person is at risk of severe withdrawal or medical emergency.
2. If there is immediate medical danger, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department.
3. Contact a licensed detox, crisis stabilization, or behavioral health provider for screening.
4. Verify insurance, VA/community care eligibility, Medicaid/Medicare status, or self-pay options.
5. Ask about transportation and admission timing.
6. Plan for step-down care after stabilization, such as residential, PHP, IOP, outpatient, recovery housing, or peer support.

### Information to collect
- Substances used
- Last use
- Withdrawal history
- Seizure history
- Medical conditions
- Psychiatric symptoms
- Insurance or funding source
- Veteran status
- Transportation needs

---

## 4. Family facing eviction or housing instability

### Primary need
Eviction prevention, legal aid, rental assistance, utility help, case management, and shelter planning if prevention fails.

### Suggested pathway
1. Determine whether there is a court date, eviction notice, or informal threat of eviction.
2. Contact legal aid as early as possible.
3. Check rental assistance, utility assistance, and emergency financial assistance resources.
4. If children are involved, check family shelter and school district homeless liaison resources.
5. Create a backup shelter and transportation plan.
6. Document deadlines, required paperwork, and agency contacts.

### Information to collect
- Current housing status
- Notice or court date
- Amount owed
- Household size
- Children present
- Income or benefits
- ID/documents available
- Transportation barriers

---

## 5. Person in mental health crisis but not an immediate 911 emergency

### Primary need
Crisis support, mobile response if available, stabilization, outpatient linkage, and safety planning.

### Suggested pathway
1. If there is immediate danger, call 911.
2. If there is suicide risk or emotional crisis, call or text 988.
3. Contact local crisis response, mobile response, or behavioral health access lines if available.
4. Identify whether the person needs inpatient stabilization, outpatient care, peer support, or case management.
5. Confirm transportation and support system.
6. Document risk level, resources contacted, and next steps.

### Key considerations
- Do not leave a person alone if they are at imminent risk.
- Encourage direct contact with crisis professionals.
- Verify program availability before referral.

---

## 6. Recently released person needing reentry support

### Primary need
ID documents, housing, food, employment, legal support, benefits, behavioral health care, and transportation.

### Suggested pathway
1. Identify immediate shelter and food needs.
2. Check for ID, Social Security card, birth certificate, and benefits status.
3. Contact reentry organizations and legal aid if applicable.
4. Screen for behavioral health, substance use, and medical needs.
5. Connect to employment, workforce, and case management services.
6. Track barriers and follow-up dates.
